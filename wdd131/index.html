Utah

Utah's history and character are largely defined by the arrival of Mormon pioneers in 1847, who settled the region seeking religious freedom and significantly shaped the state's culture. The state is renowned for its unique and dramatic landscapes, which include the Rocky Mountains, arid deserts, and five national parks famous for their canyons and arches. Since achieving statehood in 1896, Utah has evolved into a rapidly growing state with a diversified economy that includes a major technology sector, tourism, and mining. Despite its modern advancements, the profound influence of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and the state's natural beauty remain central to its identity.
